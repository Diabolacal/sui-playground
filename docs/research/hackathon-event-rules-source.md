As part of the Hackathon, You may provide to CCP work product derived from CCP Materials (as defined below in Section 8) including, without limitation, comments, stories, ideas, code or other materials and suggestions regarding EVE Frontier either during or after the Hackathon (the “Entry”).

Your Entry must be developed on or after the Hackathon start date;

Your Entry must be your own original work, not copied from other third parties without their permission;

6. How to Enter
Any team is limited to five (5) members (each, a “Team”). You cannot participate on multiple Teams. A member of a Team must register an account on the Team’s behalf for the Hackathon through Deepsurge and Entry must be submitted via same. 

All Entries must be developed and posted on GitHub. When You submit your Entry, a hyperlink to Your Team’s repository on GitHub must be included along with any materials or design drawings. 

The entry procedure and guidelines will be provided in the Hackathon announcement and are incorporated herein by reference. Any Entries not submitted in accordance with this procedure and guidelines in the Hackathon will be disregarded. 

No other methods of entry will be accepted.

11. Winner Selection
Following the Hackathon Conclusion, all eligible Entries will be judged by a panel of three qualified judges. 

In addition, members of the public will be able to vote for the ‘Best Entry’ prize through Deepsurge, subject to the voting terms provided at time of voting. Players must cast their votes by no later than 15 April 2026. Eligible player votes will account for 25% of the overall vote. Limit one (1) vote per Player. Votes received from any player in excess of the stated limitation will be void. Participants are prohibited from obtaining votes by any fraudulent or inappropriate means, including, without limitation, paying for votes, trading votes or offering prizes or other inducements to members of the public, as determined by Sponsor in its sole discretion; all such votes will be void and such Teams may be disqualified, at CCP’s discretion. 

The judges will review eligible Entries using the following judging criteria: 

Best Entry:

Concept & Feasibility -What is the core idea of the mod, what problem does it solve for players, and why would it be adopted inside the Frontier? 

Mod Design - How well is the mod designed as a system rather than a one-off feature? 

Concept Implementation - How effectively has the concept and design been translated into a working mod? 

Player Utility – Does this mod meaningfully change how players operate in the Frontier?

EVE Frontier Relevance & Vibe - Does this feel like a natural extension of EVE Frontier?

Creativity & Originality - Is this idea bold, novel, or uniquely “Frontier”?

UX & Usability - How intuitive and usable is the mod in real play?

Visual Presentation & Demo - How clearly and confidently is the mod presented?

OPTIONAL BONUS CRITERIA: Stillness Deployment - Has the mod been deployed and is it operational inside Stillness by no later than 14 days after the Hackathon Conclusion.

Bonus Prizes: 

Most Utility: Mods that materially change how players survive, coordinate, explore, or compete.

Best Technical Implementation: Clean architecture, smart use of Frontier systems, scalability, and robustness.

Most Creative: Novel ideas, clever reinterpretations, and bold system concepts that feel uniquely Frontier.

Weirdest Idea: Visually striking, surprising, or meme-worthy creations that push the Frontier in strange directions.

Best Live Frontier Integration: Mods that are deployed and functioning in Stillness, interacting with the live world and real players.

In the event of a tie for any Prize, an additional tie-breaking judge will judge all such tied Entries using applicable judging criteria to determine the winner. Sponsor reserves the right to not award all Prizes if, in its sole discretion, it does not receive a sufficient number of eligible and qualified Entries. Judging scores and votes will not be revealed.
